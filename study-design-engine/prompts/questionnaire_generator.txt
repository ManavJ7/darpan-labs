## Your Task
Generate a complete, research-ready questionnaire. The questionnaire must follow the cognitive funnel principle: broad → specific, unaided → aided, unprimed → primed.

## Section Structure (fixed section IDs, in this exact order)

### S1_screening — Screening & Qualification
- Purpose: Filter out unqualified respondents IMMEDIATELY. Every non-qualifying respondent costs money.
- MUST align with the target audience from the research design (age, gender, category usage, brand awareness, purchase behavior).
- Include both inclusion criteria (who qualifies) and exclusion criteria (e.g., work in market research, work for competitor brands).
- Use single_select with clear terminate logic.

### S2_category_context — Category Context & Current Behavior
- Purpose: Establish respondent's relationship with the category BEFORE concept exposure. This prevents the concept from framing their category perceptions.
- Include: category usage frequency, brands used/considered, satisfaction with current options, unmet needs.
- Use the funnel technique: start broad ("How often do you use [category]?") then narrow ("Which brands have you used in the past 3 months?").
- Keep this section concise — 4-6 questions maximum. Its job is context, not diagnostics.

### Per-Concept Sections: S3_concept_1, S3_concept_2, ... S3_concept_N
- Create ONE section per concept under test (from the Concepts Under Test input). The section_id MUST follow the pattern `S3_concept_1`, `S3_concept_2`, etc.
- Each per-concept section MUST contain, in this exact order:
  1. **Concept Exposure** question (type: concept_exposure) — show the concept stimulus.
  2. **Two open-text questions** — "What, if anything, did you find most appealing about this idea?" and "What, if anything, would you change or improve?" These MUST come BEFORE any scaled questions to capture unprimed reactions.
  3. **Core KPI questions** — Purchase Intent MUST be first (non-negotiable). Then overall appeal, uniqueness, relevance, believability, etc. from the selected metrics. Use 5-point behavioral scales (NOT agree/disagree).
  4. **Diagnostic questions** — attribute ratings, brand fit, value perception relevant to this concept. Randomize order within diagnostics.
- Include pipe_from references for concept rotation logic.
- Maintain identical scale types and anchor labels across all per-concept sections.

### Section Grouping Rules
- **Pre-concept group**: S1_screening, S2_category_context
- **Per-concept group**: S3_concept_1, S3_concept_2, ... S3_concept_N (one per concept)
- **Post-concept group**: S6_competitive, S7_price_sensitivity, S8_demographics

### S6_competitive — Competitive Benchmarking
- Purpose: Position the tested concept(s) against competitive alternatives.
- Include: preference vs. current brand, switching likelihood, source of volume (what would they stop buying/using?).
- Only include if competitive metrics were selected. Skip this section if not relevant.

### S7_price_sensitivity — Price Sensitivity
- Purpose: Gauge price acceptance and willingness to pay.
- If Van Westendorp or Gabor-Granger is the methodology, structure accordingly.
- For concept tests: simple price acceptance ("At [price], how likely would you be to buy?") followed by "What would you expect to pay?"
- Only include if price-related metrics were selected.

### S8_demographics — Demographics & Classification
- **MUST be the final section.** Never ask demographics before concept evaluation — it primes identity-based responding and increases early abandonment.
- Include: age (verify vs. screener), gender, income, education, household composition.
- Place sensitive questions (income, political views) at the very end within this section.

## Question Design Rules (MANDATORY)

1. **No leading or loaded questions.** Every question must be neutrally worded. No superlatives ("best", "amazing"), no assumptions ("Since you enjoy..."), no emotionally charged framing.

2. **No double-barreled questions.** Never ask about two things in one question. "How satisfied are you with the taste and packaging?" is two questions — split them.

3. **No agree/disagree scales for behavioral measures.** Agree/disagree scales introduce acquiescence bias (people tend to agree). Use direct behavioral questions instead: "How likely would you be to buy?" not "I would buy this product: Agree/Disagree."

4. **Randomize response options** where order could bias results. Mark randomize=true for attribute lists, brand lists, and any question where primacy/recency effects could apply.

5. **Attention check required.** Include one attention-check question in S4 or S5 (e.g., "Please select 'Strongly agree' for this question"). Set design_notes to "ATTENTION_CHECK".

6. **Duration target: 12-18 minutes.** If estimated duration exceeds 20 minutes, reduce diagnostic questions or consolidate scales. Cognitive fatigue begins at ~7 minutes and severely degrades data quality after 20 minutes.

7. **Mobile-first design.** Most respondents complete surveys on phones. Questions must be readable on small screens. Limit grid/matrix questions. Prefer individual rating questions.

8. **Scale consistency.** Use the same point scale (5-point recommended) throughout the KPI and diagnostic sections. Do not mix 5-point and 7-point scales.

## Question Types
- single_select — Single-choice (radio button)
- multi_select — Multiple-choice (checkbox)
- open_text — Free-text response
- rating — Scale/rating question (5-point Likert or semantic differential)
- ranking — Rank-ordering
- concept_exposure — Concept stimulus display + reaction

## Scale Format Rules
- For **Likert** rating scales: use `"scale": {"type": "likert_5pt", "options": [{"value": 1, "label": "Strongly disagree"}, ...], "anchors": {"low": "...", "high": "..."}}`.
- For **semantic differential** rating scales: you MUST include `"attribute_pairs"` — a list of bipolar adjective pairs that respondents rate the concept on. Use `"scale": {"type": "semantic_differential", "options": [{"value": 1, "label": "1"}, {"value": 2, "label": "2"}, ...{"value": 5, "label": "5"}], "attribute_pairs": [{"left": "Boring", "right": "Exciting"}, {"left": "Low Quality", "right": "High Quality"}, ...]}`. Include 4-6 attribute pairs relevant to the product category. Never leave `attribute_pairs` empty or null.
- For **single_select** / **multi_select**: always provide `"options"` with all answer choices. Never leave options empty.
