## Your Task
Design the optimal research methodology for this study. Every parameter must be justified by the study's objectives, category dynamics, and statistical requirements.

### Testing Methodology Selection
Choose the methodology based on the study objectives and concept count:
- **monadic**: Each respondent evaluates ONE concept. Gold standard — eliminates all order bias and interaction effects. Required when concepts differ substantially in category or price tier. Best for final go/no-go decisions. Expensive: total sample = n_per_concept × num_concepts.
- **sequential_monadic**: Each respondent evaluates 2-5 concepts in randomized order. Cost-efficient for screening and ranking studies. Introduces order effects (mitigated by rotation). Best when concepts are comparable and the goal is relative preference.
- **proto_monadic**: Respondent evaluates one concept in depth (monadic), then sees all concepts for forced comparison. Gives both absolute (unbiased) and relative reads. Best when you need both diagnostic depth and comparative preference.

Decision framework:
- High-stakes launch decision with 2-3 concepts → monadic
- Screening 5+ concepts → sequential_monadic
- Need both diagnostics and ranking → proto_monadic
- High-involvement categories (automotive, finance, tech) → prefer monadic (concepts need more evaluation time)
- Low-involvement categories (FMCG, beverages) → sequential_monadic works well

### Sample Size Calculation
Apply the standard formula: n = p(1-p)(z/E)² where p=0.5 (conservative), z=1.96 for 95% CI, E=margin of error.

Rules:
- Each concept needs its own adequate sample for standalone analysis.
- For monadic: total = n_per_concept × num_concepts.
- For sequential monadic: total = (n_per_concept × num_concepts) / concepts_per_respondent.
- Minimum cell sizes: n=30 for any subgroup analysis. n=100 per concept for directional reads. n=200 per concept for robust quantitative reads with subgroup analysis.
- Adjust for expected incidence rate: effective_sample = target_n / incidence_rate.
- For high-involvement categories, consider that survey completion rates may be lower.

### Confidence Level & Margin of Error
- Default: 95% confidence, ±5% margin of error (n≈385 per concept).
- High-stakes (product launch, large investment): 95% confidence, ±3% MOE (n≈1068 per concept).
- Screening/exploratory: 90% confidence, ±5% MOE (n≈271 per concept).
- Match the statistical rigor to the business decision's financial stakes.

### Rotation Design
- **latin_square**: Systematic position balancing. Each concept appears in each position equally. Use for 3-5 concepts. Only requires N orderings (where N = num_concepts).
- **balanced_incomplete_block (BIBD)**: When respondents see a subset of all concepts. Ensures each pair of concepts is evaluated by the same number of respondents. Use for 5+ concepts.
- **full_rotation**: All N! permutations. Thorough but requires large samples. Use only for ≤4 concepts with large budgets.
- **random**: Acceptable fallback when sample constraints prevent structured rotation.

### Demographic Quotas
Design quotas based on the target audience from the study brief:
- Use independent quotas for the 2-3 most important dimensions (age, gender, geography). Do NOT interlock more than 2-3 variables — this creates unfillable cells.
- Proportional quotas: match population distribution when the goal is representativeness.
- Use soft quota ranges (e.g., 48-52% instead of exactly 50%) to avoid blocking qualified respondents near end of fieldwork.
- Every segment must have a minimum target of n=30 for statistical validity.
- Standard dimensions by industry:
  - FMCG: age, gender, SEC, urban/rural
  - Automotive: age, gender, income, car ownership status
  - Tech: age, gender, tech adoption segment
  - Luxury: age, gender, income/affluence tier

### Data Collection Method
- **online_panel**: Standard, fast, cost-effective. Best for urban, digitally literate audiences.
- **mobile_survey**: For mobile-first markets (India, SEA) or younger demographics.
- **capi** (computer-assisted personal interview): For complex stimuli requiring guided viewing, or low-literacy audiences.

### Survey Language
- Recommend based on geography and audience from the study brief.
- Default: ["english"]. For India: ["english", "hindi"]. For multi-country: include local languages.

### Field Duration Estimation
- Online panel: 5-10 days for standard quotas, 10-15 days for niche audiences.
- Factor in incidence rate: low incidence (<20%) extends fieldwork.
- High-involvement categories may need longer field periods for adequate sample.
