=== NARRATIVE & STYLE EXTRACTION ===

In addition to signals, extract the following to build a richer twin:

1. **narrative_snippets**: Quote-worthy phrases that reveal personality, stories, or strong opinions. Tag each with a category:
   - "anecdote" — a specific episode or story ("Last year I quit my job to...")
   - "self_description" — how they see themselves ("I'm the kind of person who...")
   - "rule_of_thumb" — personal heuristics ("I never buy anything without sleeping on it")
   - "preference_statement" — clear preference expression ("I'd always rather have quality over quantity")
   - "emotional_reveal" — moments of vulnerability or strong emotion ("That really frustrated me because...")

2. **style_markers**: How the person communicates, not what they say. Examples:
   - "uses_analogies" — explains through comparisons
   - "hedges_frequently" — uses "maybe", "I guess", "kind of", "pretty much"
   - "gives_concrete_numbers" — provides specific figures and quantities
   - "verbose" — gives long, detailed responses
   - "terse" — gives short, minimal responses
   - "storyteller" — naturally tells stories/anecdotes
   - "systematic_thinker" — organizes thoughts in lists or categories

3. **exceptions_mentioned**: "Except when...", "Unless...", "But not for..." patterns. These are gold for realistic twin behavior.

4. **contradiction_candidates**: If this answer seems to contradict something from previous_answers, note it. E.g., "Said they're risk-averse but describes quitting a stable job impulsively."

5. **self_descriptors**: Any "I'm...", "I tend to be...", "I consider myself..." phrases. Extract the full phrase.

6. **open_loops**: Topics the user hinted at but didn't fully explore. E.g., "mentioned a startup role but didn't explain why they left." These become future question targets.

7. **exemplar_quality**: Rate 0.0-1.0 how good this answer is for twin training:
   - 0.0-0.3: Vague, generic, could be anyone
   - 0.4-0.6: Some specificity, but missing concrete details or episodes
   - 0.7-0.8: Good — specific, personal, contains at least one concrete detail
   - 0.9-1.0: Excellent — vivid, specific, contains anecdotes, exceptions, or emotional depth
