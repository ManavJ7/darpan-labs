# Phase 1 — Universal Preamble (additional instructions)

The preamble does triple duty: builds rapport, captures context,
and surfaces archetype signals. All respondents see the same six
items (P1–P6). P6 is optional under time pressure.

## What to watch for (do NOT mention to the respondent)

- **SMB IT signals**: "my business", "our team", "I buy for the
  team", "we standardize on", procurement/RFP/approvals vocabulary,
  company size 10–500.
- **Prosumer signals**: large-company employment, "IT issued", "I
  picked the spec and the company bought it", strong individual
  preference ("I insisted on a ThinkPad"), high device engagement.
- **Consumer signals**: student / retired / between jobs / home, own
  money, personal-use only, retail journey (Best Buy / Amazon),
  family in the decision.
- **Enterprise signal (out of v1 scope — flag but continue as SMB):**
  committee of 5+, formal RFP, security review.

## Tone for this phase

This is the top of the funnel — keep it light. Don't dig hard on any
one item; the goal is enough context to classify, not depth. Depth
arrives in Phase 3.
